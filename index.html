So in-short what actually happens in the camps?

The main objective is fun, educational exciting activities for the children. Its like a super fun science class, better than that at school, its all about engaging and inspiring children, with a lifelong love of science and learning through hands on activities, exciting demonstrations and experiments, team games and fun quizzes. We start off by introducing the topic, we do fun demonstrations and experiments followed by hands on activities where the children will make something to take home with them. We also provide a parent handout which describes exactly what has been covered in each session with further areas for ideas to explore at home!

Do I have to book a whole week or can I book single days?

The Camp is designed to be a week-long educational programme for maximum benefit to your child and is more cost-effective we have a limited amount individual days at a premium rate per day and no discount is applicable.

Category: Holiday Camp FAQ
a

Do you split the age groups?

No our camp is all about working together on science projects as a Team and individually! It is not like a school or a Tuition Club. We always run our camps with a tried-and-tested Mixed Age Dynamic which works really well because it gives extra responsibility to the older children and helps stretch the younger children. Although our camps are open to kids aged 5-12 the majority of children tend to be ages 5-8 yrs.

What is the Child to Instructor Classroom Ratio?

We always operate on a 1-10 Presenter to Child ratio. There is always a minimum of 2 Presenter on site at all times. We have one main presenter and an assistant who is there especially to look after the younger children. During term time most of our presenters run after school science clubs so are well used to working with children of that age.

What Safety Standards do you have in place?

Ensuring every Childs safety and security is our top priority. All our staff have passed enhanced police check by the Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) and all have to pass our Child Safeguarding Policy. We risk-assess every experiment and activity, and fully train the instructors who deliver the sessions. We also run a strict signing-in/out verbal collection password (VCP) policy so that only persons nominated by you are permitted to collect your child.

Do you provide food or refreshments?

No, but we do provide access to fresh drinking water to keep them hydrated during the day. Children need to bring their own water bottle (important) packed lunch, mid-session snack. We have a no sharing food policy. We respectfully ask for nut products (such as peanut butter, Nutella and bags of nuts) not to be placed in pack lunches.

What topics are you running in that week?

Do you do a free trial sessions My child is very shy?

No, but dont worry, this is a common concern from parents but from our experience you have nothing to worry about. No child is left alone, and we encourage all children to be social with each other in small groups. We always have an assistant who is there to look after the younger children and most children come back to our camps year after year. Even if there are settling-in issues, we’ll be in touch with you on how they are getting on and how to ensure they enjoy themselves. You can book for a single day by request.
